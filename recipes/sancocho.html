Sancocho Recipe

Ingredients

  • 1 lb beef stew meat
  • 1 lb pork chunks
  • 1 lb chicken pieces
  • 1 Dominican sausage (longaniza), sliced
  • 1 green plantain, sliced
  • 1 yuca (cassava), peeled and chopped
  • 1 yautía, peeled and chopped
  • 2 potatoes, chopped
  • 1 cup pumpkin (auyama), cubed
  • 1 ear of corn, cut into pieces
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bunch cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon oregano
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Black pepper to taste
  • Water

Steps

  1. In a large heavy pot, heat oil over medium heat.
  2. Season the meats with salt, pepper, oregano, and garlic.
  3. Brown the beef and pork first, then add the chicken and sausage.
  4. Add onion, bell pepper, and tomato paste. Stir and cook for a few minutes.
  5. Add enough water to cover everything and bring to a boil.
  6. Reduce heat and simmer for about 30–40 minutes until meats begin to tenderize.
  7. Add plantain, yuca, yautía, potatoes, corn, and pumpkin.
  8. Continue simmering until all root vegetables are soft and the stew thickens.
  9. Lightly mash a few pieces of pumpkin or plantain in the pot to naturally thicken the broth.
  10. Add chopped cilantro during the last few minutes of cooking.
  11. Adjust salt and pepper to taste before serving.
  12. Serve hot with white rice and avocado on the side.
