Mangu Recipe

Description

Mangú is one of the most iconic breakfast dishes of the Dominican Republic, traditionally served as part of “Los Tres Golpes” — mashed green plantains topped with sautéed red onions and served alongside fried cheese, fried salami, and fried eggs. The plantains are boiled and mashed until smooth and creamy, creating a soft, comforting base with a mild flavor that pairs perfectly with savory toppings. Mangú is hearty, affordable, and deeply tied to Dominican culture and family breakfasts.

Ingredients

  • 4 green plantains
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 3 tablespoons butter or olive oil
  • ½ cup warm water (from boiling plantains)
  • 1 red onion, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil

Steps

  1. Peel the green plantains and cut them into halves or thick chunks.
  2. Place them in a pot with enough salted water to cover and boil until very tender.
  3. While they boil, heat olive oil in a pan and sauté the sliced red onions over medium heat.
  4. Add vinegar to the onions, stir, and cook until soft and slightly pink. Set aside.
  5. Drain the plantains, reserving some of the hot cooking water.
  6. Mash the plantains in a bowl while still hot.
  7. Add butter or olive oil and a little of the reserved warm water.
  8. Continue mashing until smooth and creamy, adding more water if needed to reach desired consistency.
  9. Serve topped with the sautéed onions.
  10. Optional: Serve with fried cheese, Dominican salami, and fried eggs for the full “Tres Golpes” experience.
